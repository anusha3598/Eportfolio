Image Editing

Book Cover

PART 1

For this project, I had the exciting opportunity to design a book cover of my choice, and I selected *The Devil Wears Prada* by Lauren Weisberger. This novel delves into themes of ambition, personal growth, and relationships through the journey of Andrea Sachs, an assistant to the formidable Miranda Priestly at *Runway* magazine. I created the design using Canva, leveraging its intuitive tools to bring my vision to life. I opted for a minimalist black background with a subtle gradient, reflecting the elegance and sophistication of the fashion industry. The title, *The Devil Wears Prada*, is styled in bold red to symbolize power and intensity, while the author’s name is presented in a smaller, refined font to maintain balance. To emphasize the novel's widespread acclaim, I added a "#1 New York Times Bestseller" badge. The main image features a stylish woman representing Miranda Priestly, adorned with devil horns and a tail to hint at her complex and commanding nature. The layout prioritizes a clean and balanced composition, harmonizing the title, imagery, and author’s name. Careful use of white space enhances the overall sophistication and minimalist aesthetic of the cover.

Logo

PART 2

I designed a logo for a café I hope to open, combining my passion for coffee and nature into a visually appealing and meaningful design. The logo features a steaming coffee cup with a blooming flower emerging from it, symbolizing growth, freshness, and the nurturing environment I envision for the café. The color palette is carefully chosen to represent the café’s essence: earthy brown tones evoke the richness of coffee, while soft blue accents add a touch of freshness and tranquility. The name *Bloom & Brew* is styled in bold, modern typography, exuding confidence and warmth. Beneath it, "Coffee Shop" appears in a smaller, elegant font to balance the design and provide clarity.
